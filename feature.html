My programming journey - a recap

A series of constant failures, learnings and I can't believe I am saying this but joy too.

I started my programming journey in 2019. Oh, wait - one disclaimer.

My husband is a passionate computer programmer. Along with that he has all the right ticks on his profile:

-- One of the best colleges (i.e) IIT. In India getting into IIT is similar to getting into MIT. But only tougher (Acceptance rate of IIT is one percent while for MIT it is eight percent. Please do a simple google search, if you don't believe me).

-- Couple of FANG experiences,

-- and the best aspect, a great payscale.

So, yeah. His pay scale really inspired me to pursue computer programming as a career. This pursuant had been an on and off journey. I usually don't count this time but I started programming in 2017 with some python book my husband suggested. I also started the python for everybody specialization by Charles Russell Severance. But I think in a month, the interest eroded and quit on both of them.

In 2019, I left a job in online marketing and advertising and again started pursuing computer programming. Again, I relied on my husband to help me. I again started with books, and again, I left programming!

In mid 2020, I decided to pursue this journey through a University. I thought of getting into a good university programme which could float my boat in that direction. I think my ideology at that time was if I can't do it on my own then following a well structured system would help.

So, I started my computer science journey from a university reference. I understood from the entrance requirements that I need to brush up my math skills. This means going back to calculus and precalculus, which I haven't touched from the past 12 years. In hindsight, I believe that I made a huge mistake. Instead of focusing on learning practical skills, like HTML, CSS, Javascript, React, API's etc or in short- front end development, I spent my time and effort learning skills which are not directly transferable in programming jobs.

I wish I could go back in time and redo those decisions. But it is okay. No good learning and knowledge goes bad.

But still, I wish!

Anyway, the last two to three years were really confusing. I knew what I was doing might be useful for a university curriculum, but that is just about it. Anyway I kept following the entrance requirements, which means taking general introductory CS courses online.

I was preparing myself to start my programming through a University but to my utter surprise, the University rejected me. And I was shattered. Oh, I have been rejected twice by now lol. But the first time was the hardest. In my mind I was already accepted into the programme and have made lofty plans. The rejection was really hard to accept. From that point onwards, I think from mid 2021, I decided to put all my energy into getting accepted. It was more about getting into the programme now for me. So, I decided to take credit courses for Calculus. I also started the university's first year course online, which means learning a functional language name racket. This experience tested my grit and patience. There have been so many times along the way where I believed programming is not for me. I can't do it. But getting into university was everything. So I persevere. It took a lot of time but things started connecting. Though, this entire experience is not useful for jobs, but I don't know, I started enjoying programming, maybe!

Or maybe what I was enjoying more was being busy. This learning experience also made me realize how depressed I was for a very long time. All this experience at least gave me goals and a daily routine to pursue those goals. So, it could be I was just enjoying doing something good.

Of Course all this time spent will never contribute towards an actual job, but what I had was a structure or routine which I unknowingly designed.

I think somewhere in the beginning of 2022, I started having doubts again about my direction. I had already applied again to the university but this time, I was scared. I kept asking myself what will happen if I am rejected again. I know I am learning computer science, but I didn't feel like taking that direction anymore to learn programming, mostly because it wasn't giving me useful programming skills. The pursuit of getting into a degree programme could be longer that I am expecting and it doesn't end afterwards. Even if I get into the programme, I don't know if I will get the best internship or job. This time around I had been really active on reddit too. I was reading posts of people who may have had the best degrees but not necessarily the job they wanted. So, I was really screaming from the inside!

From reading multiple experiences, I had understood that learning front end skills are crucial. You could be with a degree or without degree, but these skills are most important in assessing candidates. Also, thanks to my husband's understanding of the industry, I knew javascript is an important language for jobs. Knowing what I want to learn is clear but I didn't know how to learn it. I tried books and I failed to learn from them. So, I knew I had to find another way to learn.

For a few nights, I was sleepless. I remember digging google and frankly relied on it to help me from my misery. I remember reading articles about people who were following the Free Code Camp syllabus and learning how to program. FCC was free and easy to get onboard too. I started their syllabus and completed their first certification. I can't explain the kind of confidence it gave me. I was sure from that point that I will follow their syllabus to start my front end development journey.

While completing their javascript and data structures certificate, one of the best certificates in my opinion, I found Scrimba. Their front end path is bible to learning front end technologies and programming. I am pursuing their front end path and planning to complete it. It is a great resource. I am Currently on Module 7. Scrimba has 14 modules in the front end development path.

Simultaneously, I have started trying my hands on leetcode, all thanks to the FCC javascript course, which gave me confidence to start leetcoding. I am also doing a data structure javascript course through udemy to become better in leetcoding. This journey is slow though. I think it would take me years of consistent practice to really be good at solving problems.

Right now I am in October of 2022. All in all, I feel that this experience is a ride I will cherish always. It is still a long journey but this time around I am sure of my direction (At Least for now! lol). All I have to do is follow it.

Year 2022, thank you!
